The history of the Coffee House at College Street can be traced to albert hall,which was founded in April 1876. This Albert Hall was the primary residence of Ramkaml Sen,Treasurer of the Bank of Bengal and Secretary of thr Asiatic Sociaty,Calcutta, in the early 19th century.Later,the Coffee Board decided to start a coffee joint from the Albert Hallin 1942.Notable citizens were frequent visitors to the to the place. in 1947,the Central Government changed the name of the place to ,"Coffee House".The place became a meeting place for the poets, artistes,literati and people from thw world of art and culture. in 1958, the management decided to shut doen the Coffee House ,but it was re-opened the same year,after professors of Presidency college and Calcutta University rushed off a special petition to the government ,to save the heritage place .in 2006,a huge financial crunch kept the co-operatie society from undertaking renovation of the coffe house.Though a few companies such as asian paints apporoached the society with offfers to renovate the restaurent,the offers were refused due to clash of norms and,conditions.
